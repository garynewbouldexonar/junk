ATTACHMENT C

Methodology To Assess Available Transmission Capability

Cinergy will respond to a firm point-to-point transmission service request by performing studies to determine if sufficient transmission capacity is available to accommodate the request. These studies will consist of an evaluation of the thermal interconnection capability, the calculation of system transfer capability, and an analysis of the configuration of the interconnected system. The transfer capacity available will be the remaining capacity after accounting for the Transmission Provider's reliability requirements to serve its Native Load Customers, the rights of joint owners of the Transmission System under joint transmission ownership agreements, and prior contractual commitments including any integration service contract(s) previously filed. The study will analyze the impact of the proposed transmission request on the thermal capability and voltage profile of the transmission system, considering the reliability requirements and commitments discussed.

Cinergy will adhere to Good Utility Practice, NERC Guidelines, and ECAR procedures and criteria when conducting the studies. In addition, Cinergy will have its own criteria, which will be designed to minimize customer outages and prevent equipment damage during probable contingencies. The transfer capabilities will be computed based on applicable NERC Guides.

The Transmission Provider will also adhere to the ECAR procedures that guide the calculation of transfer capacities that may be found in the most recent ECAR Assessment of Transmission System Performance.

The system performance will be evaluated by the Transmission Provider utilizing ECAR power flow models. These models are developed by ECAR member systems and include firm transfers in the base case. The transfer capacity computed will be incremental to the base case values. Normally, Cinergy will utilize ECAR models that have been modified to provide a more detailed representation of the Cinergy system. The Transmission Provider will make these modified cases available upon request.

ECAR prepares load flow models that represent system conditions up to 10 years in the future. The actual series of models developed during any particular year are determined by mutual agreement. Cinergy will select the power flow models that most closely align with the commencement date and the period of the requested Transmission Service to analyze the impact of the proposed transmission usage.

The Transmission Provider will use commercially available power system analysis software packages to conduct the studies. The Transmission Provider will use at a minimum, the line outage lists used by ECAR for operating studies for the upcoming seasonal peaks. Transfer capabilities calculated utilizing a linear load flow analysis package will be verified with AC analysis studies to determine if any voltage problems occur or lines overload due to reactive power flows. The transfer capability studies may also be supplemented with dynamic studies if the system appears to be approaching a stability constraint.

The Transmission Provider will compute the First Contingency Incremental Transfer Capability (FCITC) from the delivering party to the receiving party. This will be used to determine the maximum amount of power that may be transferred (incremental to the transfer modeled in the base case) with the most critical transmission line out of service and transmission line loadings and voltages within their respective emergency ratings prior to operator directed system adjustments. If operating procedures exist that will increase the transfer capability, the transfer capability will be computed assuming that the procedure is in effect. However, if the constraint or the operating procedure is in another control area, the purchaser of the transmission service and the Transmission Provider will need to contact the other control area to determine the general availability of the procedure and to help evaluate the constraint.

The methodology used to calculate the transfer capability calculation will depend on the service requested. The methodology utilized is:

A. Purchase from a specified unit(s)

For this case the FCITC will be determined by

increasing the power from the specific unit designated by the purchaser as its power supply and either 1) increasing the receiving area load or 2) decreasing the receiving area generation. The selection of 1) or 2) will depend on the transaction being evaluated.

B. Purchase from a Control Area

In this case the FCITC will be calculated by increasing generation based on the projected operation of the systems when considering the transfer and either increasing receiving area load or decreasing receiving area generation.

C. Sales

FCITC modeling power sales will be calculated similarly to the purchases above.

If the studies determine that a constraint occurs in another control area not participating in the transaction, the Transmission Provider and the purchaser of the transmission service will need to work with the appropriate parties to determine if the limitation is valid and what facility additions may be required to support the transfer. The purchaser has the option to reduce the request to a level that alleviates the constraint.

Studies will be conducted utilizing several different base cases. The first case will be the normal base case which is the ECAR base case with appropriate detail added. The second case will be a reliability import base case, in which different base load generators are reduced to zero MW and imports are scheduled into the Cinergy Control Area to make up for the outage generation. The outage generation will be replaced by modeling imports from surrounding control areas based on their ability to supply the power. The FCITC values calculated from the reliability import case allow for the outage of various generating resources.

The transfer capacity that will be considered to be available is the lowest of: (1) the lowest value from the FCITC calculation, (2) the reliability import case, (3) the thermal limits of the facilities that form the interface between Cinergy and either party, less any amount for existing transactions. In any case, the outage of direct interconnects will result in the loss of the ability to transfer power.

The Transmission Provider may also perform simulations for off-peak conditions if study is required to determine limitations.

Cinergy will perform all studies related to Transmission Service requests as it performs transmission studies for its own use of the system. However, as a practical matter, it must be noted that planning studies must gauge the performance of the system based on a limited number of simulations. In actual daily operation of the system the limits as determined in the transfer capability study may vary due to system conditions.

ARTICLE III

METERING AND DATA ACQUISITION

3.1 Metering - The electric power received from resources and delivered under the Tariff and this Service Agreement shall be determined from measurements taken at, or adjusted to measurements taken at such resources and the Customer's Network Load, or from a schedule of delivered quantities. Suitable metering equipment shall include standard types of electric meters, potential and current transformers, and such other appurtenances as shall be necessary to give the instantaneous values of kilowatts and kilovars, and an automatic record of kilowatt-hours for each clock hour. When there is a possibility of flows of electricity in either direction, then metering equipment shall be installed to provide metering data for each direction of flow. The specific types of metering equipment, the details of the metering arrangement and the records to be kept shall be determined by the Parties. The timing devices of all meters having such devices shall be maintained in time synchronism as closely as practicable. The meters shall be sealed and the seals shall be broken only upon occasions when the meters are to be tested or adjusted.

3.2 Testing - The aforesaid metering equipment shall be tested by the Party providing the same at suitable intervals and its accuracy of registration maintained in accordance with then current utility industry practice. On request of either Party, a special test may be made at the expense of the Party requesting such special test. Representatives of both of the Parties shall be afforded an opportunity to be present at all routine or special tests and upon occasions when any readings, for purposes of settlements, are taken from meters not bearing an automatic record.

If any test of metering equipment shall disclose any inaccuracy exceeding two percent (2%), the accounts between the Parties for service theretofore delivered pursuant to this Service Agreement shall be adjusted to correct for the inaccuracy disclosed over the shorter of the following two periods: (1) for the thirty (30)-day period immediately preceding the day of the test; or (2) for the period that such inaccuracy may be determined to have existed. Should the metering equipment as provided hereunder at any time fail to register, the electric power and energy delivered shall be determined from the best available data.

For the purpose of checking the records of the metering equipment installed by one of the Parties as hereinabove provided, the other Party shall have the right to install check metering equipment at the metering points. Metering equipment so installed by one Party on the premises of the other Party, unless otherwise provided for in this Service Agreement, shall be owned and maintained by the Party installing such equipment. Upon termination of this Service Agreement, the Party owning such metering equipment shall remove it from the premises of the other Party. Authorized representatives of both Parties shall have access at all reasonable hours to the premises where the meters are located and to the records made by the meters.

3.3 Data Acquisition - Service under the Tariff and this Service Agreement shall not commence until the Customer and Transmission Provider have installed all metering equipment, data acquisition facilities, communication equipment and associated equipment necessary to provide the metered data at the various locations of the Network Resources and Network Loads to the Transmission Provider's system control center in a manner consistent with applicable NERC and ECAR guidelines for the reliable operation of the Bulk Transmission System and the Common Transmission Systems.

Introduction

When a Security Coordinator identifies or is informed that transmission facilities within his Security Area are about to exceed, or have exceeded, their Operating Security Limit, the Security Coordinator shall, at his discretion, use either:

  • A "local" transmission loading relief procedure if that procedure includes the transmission system with the Constraint, or
  • The NERC Transmission Loading Relief Procedure as detailed in this Appendix.

The NERC Transmission Loading Relief Procedure provides Security Coordinators with an orderly process to maintain or return transmission equipment loading to within Operating Security Limits. The Procedure includes nine Levels:

Level 1 – Notify Security Coordinators of potential Operating Security Limit violations

Level 2a – Hold additional INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs to prevent Operating Security Limit violations

Level 2b – Reallocate Firm Transmission Service

Level 2c – Reallocate Non-firm Transmission Service

Level 3 – Curtail INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs served by Non-firm Transmission Service Arrangements

Level 4 – Reconfigure and Redispatch

Level 5 – Curtail INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs served by Firm Transmission Service Arrangements

Level 6 – Implement Emergency Procedures

Level 0 – TLR Concluded

Holding INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs

If an Operating Security Limit violation is imminent, Security Coordinators may direct their Control Areas to neither change nor confirm additional INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs, as identified by the Security Coordinators, in a manner that will aggravate the condition (for example, changing the transmission contract path but not the actual source and sink).

Curtailing INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs

Should curtailment become necessary, all INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs, including INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs between adjacent control areas, whose PTDF across the specific constrained facility are equal to or greater than a 5% shall be curtailed whenever practicable on a proportional basis and according to these Procedures (See Section A., "NERC Transmission Loading Relief Levels," and Section D, "Transaction Curtailment Formula.")

Curtailment of INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs follows the FERC pro forma tariff with regard to Transmission Service priorities:

Priority 1. Service over secondary receipt and delivery points – NS

Priority 2. Hourly Service – NH

Priority 3. Daily Service – ND

Priority 4. Weekly Service – NW

Priority 5. Monthly Service – NM>

Priority 6. Non-firm imports for native load and network customers from sources not designated as network resources – NN

Priority 7. Firm Service – F

INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION Priority on the Contract Path

If the INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION is served by two or more consecutive Transmission Providers ("links") on a contract path, the curtailment priority will be equal to the lowest Transmission Service priority arranged for over the links on the path. However, any INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION that is served by Firm Transmission Service will be considered as a Firm Transaction if the constraint is on that Transmission Firm Service link.

Emergency Procedures

NERC recognizes that Transmission Providers are subject to obligations under FERC-approved tariffs and other agreements, and nothing in these procedures should be interpreted as changing those obligations. However, if the transmission loading condition is deemed critical to bulk system reliability by a Security Coordinator, the Security Coordinator has the authority to immediately direct the control areas to redispatch generation or reduce load to mitigate the critical condition until INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs can be reduced utilizing the TLR Procedures, or other methods, to return the system to a reliable state. All Control Areas shall comply with all requests from their Security Coordinator.

Return to Normal Operations

The Security Coordinator that is experiencing the constraint within its Security Area shall notify all Security Coordinators when the adverse conditions are mitigated and the system is in a "normal" state.

 

  1. Transmission Loading Relief (TLR) Levels

Introduction

The NERC Transmission Loading Relief Procedure is a nine-level process for managing INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs and mitigating Operating Security Limit violations. Security Coordinators must keep the Transmission Providers informed when entering and leaving these TLR levels.

The Security Coordinators may not necessarily follow these TLR Levels in their numerical order. Furthermore, if a Security Coordinator deems that a transmission loading condition could jeopardize bulk system reliability, the Security Coordinator has the authority to immediately direct the Control Areas to redispatch generation or reduce load to mitigate the critical condition until INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs can be reduced utilizing the TLR Procedures, or other methods, to return the system to a secure state.

The TLR Procedure is guided by information obtained from the interim Interchange Distribution Calculator (iIDC). Security Coordinators must also treat known INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs that may not appear in the iIDC in accordance with the procedures in this document.

Level 1 – Notify Security Coordinators of potential Operating Security Limit Violations

  • The transmission system is secure.
  • The Security Coordinator foresees a transmission contingency or other operating problem that could cause one or more transmission facilities to exceed their Operating Security Limit.

The Security Coordinator shall notify all Security Coordinators as soon as the condition is observed. All affected Security Coordinators shall check to ensure that INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs are posted in the (Interim) Interchange Distribution Calculator.

Level 2a – Hold transfers to prevent Operating Security Limit Violations
  • The transmission system is secure,
  • One or more transmission facilities are expected to approach, or are approaching, or are at their Operating Security Limit.
  • The Security Coordinator will assist the Control Areas and Transmission Providers to ensure that the loading on these Constrained Facilities does not increase, and not allow additional INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs that increase the loading on the Constrained Facilities based on a 5% or greater Power Transfer Distribution Factor (PTDF).

During Level 2a, new INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs, whether pre-scheduled or not, will not be allowed to begin, nor will existing INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs be allowed to increase if they increase the loading on the Constrained Facilities based on a 5% or greater PTDF.

NOTE: The Security Coordinator should allow INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs that reduce the loading on the Constrained Facilities.

The Security Coordinator may then proceed to reallocate Firm Transmission service (TLR Level 2b), and then Non-firm service (Level 2c)

Level 2b – Reallocation of Firm Transmission Service
  • The transmission system is secure
  • One or more transmission facilities are expected to approach, or are approaching, or are at their Operating Security Limit
  • All new INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION requests with a 5% or greater PTDF over the Constrained Facilities are being held.

At this point, the Security Coordinator shall, using best efforts, begin to reallocate the use of the transmission system to:

  • Allow a pre-scheduled INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION with a pre-existing Firm Transmission Service reservation to begin, and, as necessary, curtail INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs with Non-firm reservations for those INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs that impact the Constrained Facilities, even if those INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs are already flowing.
    •
  • Allow as soon as practicable a non-pre-scheduled INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION with a pre-existing Firm reservation to begin, and, as necessary, curtail INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs with Non-firm Reservations that impact the Constrained Facilities, even if those INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs are already flowing.

And then:
  • If all INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs with Non-firm Reservations have been curtailed, the Security Coordinator shall ask the Transmission Provider(s) providing Firm Transmission Service over the Constrained Facilities to redispatch generation or reconfigure transmission on their system, or arrange for redispatch or reconfiguration on other transmission systems if such agreements are in place, to mitigate the constraint to allow INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs with Firm Reservations to begin.
  • Displace, by curtailment on a pro-rata basis (based on the transmission reservation), other INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs using Firm Transmission service that contribute to the loading of the Constrained Facilities.

NOTE: When displacing other INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs, the Security Coordinator may, at his option, ask for these curtailments solely from the Transmission Providers within the Security Coordinator’s Security Area, or may turn to other Security Coordinators for assistance from Transmission Providers in their Security Areas. All INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION changes or curtailments must be entered into the IDC.

Level 2c – Reallocation of Non-Firm Transmission Service
  • The transmission system is secure
  • One or more transmission facilities are expected to approach, or are approaching, or are at their Operating Security Limit
  • All INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION requests using Firm Transmission service have been accommodated
  • All new INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION requests with a 5% or greater PTDF over the Constrained Facilities using Non-firm Transmission service are being held

At this point, the Security Coordinator shall, using best efforts, begin to reallocate the use of the transmission system to:

  • Allow a pre-scheduled INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION with a Non-firm Transmission Service reservation to begin, and, as necessary, curtail INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs of lower priority for those INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs that impact the Constrained Facilities, even if those INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs are already flowing.
  • Displace, by curtailment on a pro-rata basis (based on the transmission reservation), other INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs using the same priority transmission service that contribute to the loading on the Constrained Facilities.

Level 3 – Curtail INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs using Non-Firm Transmission Service Arrangements
  • One or more Transmission Facilities are exceeding or expecting to exceed their Operating Security Limit, or
  • One or more Transmission Facilities will exceed their Operating Security Limit upon the removal of service of another transmission facility

INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs with a 5% or greater PTDF over the Constraints using Non-Firm Transmission Service are curtailed in the following order. The two-character abbreviations correspond to Transmission Service on the Transaction Information Template ("tag").

Priority 1. Service over secondary receipt and delivery points – NS

Priority 2. Hourly Service – NH

Priority 3. Daily Service – ND

Priority 4. Weekly Service – NW

Priority 5. Monthly Service – NM

Priority 6. Non-firm imports for native load and network customers from sources not designated as network resources – NN

Level 4 – Reconfigure and Redispatch

  • One or more Transmission Facilities are above their Operating Security Limit.
  • All Non-firm INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs using Non-firm Transmission Service have been curtailed.

Following the curtailment of all INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs that impact the Constrained Facilities served by Non-Firm Transmission Service in Level 3 above, the Security Coordinator(s) and affected Transmission Providers will determine whether or not the Operating Security Limit violation has been sufficiently relieved. If INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs served by Firm Transmission Service continue to contribute to the Constrained Facilities, and before proceeding to the Curtailment of INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs using Firm Transmission Service Arrangements in Level 5 below, certain Transmission Providers may be obligated to redispatch generation or reconfigure transmission on their system, or arrange for redispatch or reconfiguration on other transmission systems, to mitigate the constraint. Specific details are explained in Section C., "Principles for Mitigating Constraints On and Off the Contract Path."

Level 5 – Curtail INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs using Firm Transmission Service Arrangements
  • One or more Transmission Facilities are above their Operating Security Limit.
  • All Non-firm INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs using Non-firm Transmission Service have been curtailed.
  • No further generation redispatch or transmission reconfiguration is possible or effective.

At this point, the Security Coordinator shall curtail INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs with a 5% or greater PTDF over the Constrained Facilities using Firm Transmission Service on a pro-rata basis until the Operating Security Limit violation has been mitigated.

Priority 7. Firm Service – F

Level 6 – Implement emergency procedures
  • One or more Transmission Facilities are above their Operating Security Limit.

If the transmission loading condition is deemed critical to bulk system reliability by a Security Coordinator, the Security Coordinator has the authority to immediately direct the Control Areas to redispatch generation or reduce load to mitigate the critical condition until INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs can be reduced utilizing the TLR Procedures or other procedures to return the system to a secure state. All Control Areas shall comply with all requests from their Security Coordinator.

Level 0 – TLR concluded

The Security Coordinator of the Security Area that is experiencing the problem shall notify all Security Coordinators when the adverse conditions are mitigated and the system is in a "normal" state. The Security Coordinator may now reestablish INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs at his discretion.

C. Principles for Mitigating Constraints On and Off the Contract Path

Introduction

Reserving transmission service for an INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION along a "contract path" may not reflect the actual distribution of the power flows over the transmission network from generation source to load sink. INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs arranged over a contract path may, therefore, overload transmission elements on other electrically parallel paths. The Security Coordinators must agree on how the NERC Transmission Loading Relief Procedure will handle these INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs to, first, ensure the operational security of the Interconnection and, second, respect the obligations of the Transmission Provider. It is also important to agree on the obligations of the Transmission Providers on and off the contract path regarding reconfiguring their transmission systems and redispatching their generation to mitigate an overload on their own system, and others’.

Refer to examples on following page.

For Constraints on the Contract Path
  1. A Transmission Provider providing non-firm Transmission Service is not obligated to arrange for generation redispatch or transmission reconfiguration to mitigate constraints on its transmission system. The transaction is considered non-firm, even if other links in the contract path are firm, and takes on the lowest transmission service priority along the contract path.

    Discussion. The Transmission Provider simply has to call its Security Coordinator for TLR and allow the curtailments to progress until the relief is realized. Firm Transmission Service links elsewhere in the contract path do not obligate Transmission Providers providing non-firm service to treat the transaction as firm. For curtailment purposes, the INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION’s priority will be the lowest priority Transmission Service on its contract path unless the constraint was on a link for which Firm Transmission Service was arranged. (See Principle #2 below.)
  2. A Transmission Provider providing Firm Transmission Service is obligated to redispatch its generation or reconfigure its transmission system to mitigate constraints on its transmission system before curtailing firm INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs that are called for under the TLR. This obligation exists regardless of whether the other links in the contract path are firm or non-firm. Furthermore, for curtailment purposes an INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION will be considered Firm if the constraint is on a link for which Firm Transmission Service was arranged.
    Discussion. The Transmission Provider providing Firm Transmission Service is obligated to transfer the energy from its point of receipt to its point of delivery regardless of the arrangements the Transmission Customer has made with other Transmission Providers along the contract path. However, other Transmission Providers along the firm path may redispatch or reconfigure at the request of the Transmission Provider providing Firm Transmission Service with the constraint if agreements are in place to do so.

For Constraints off the Contract Path
  1. A Transmission Provider providing Non-Firm Transmission Service is not obligated to redispatch generation or reconfigure transmission to mitigate Constraints on any transmission system off the contract path unless there are agreements in place to curtail or redispatch on-system INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs.

Discussion. An INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION arranged over a contract path where one or more individual links consist of Non-firm Transmission Service is considered a non-firm INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION for Constraints off the contract path. All such INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs with a 5% or greater PTDF over the Constraints will be curtailed to relieve a Constraint off the contract path before any firm INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs are curtailed. The priority level for curtailment purposes will be the lowest level of transmission service arranged for on the contract path.

Firm links in a transmission contract path do not make the entire path firm, they only ensure that the Transmission Providers providing those firm links will treat the INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION as firm with respect to their own system. The link with the lowest level priority defines the priority for the INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION.
  1. A INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION arranged over a contract path where all the individual links are provided as Firm Transmission Service is considered a firm INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION and will not be curtailed to relieve a Constraint off the contract path until all non-firm INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs with a 5% or greater PTDF over the Constraints have been curtailed. The Security Coordinator will determine which Control Areas will arrange for redispatch or reconfiguration to mitigate the Constraint.

    Discussion. If the entire contract path is Firm, then the TLR procedure will treat the INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION as firm even for Constraints off the contract path and will not curtail that INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION until all non-firm INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs have been curtailed. However, Transmission Providers off the contract path are not obligated to reconfigure or redispatch their systems unless special arrangements are in place. Because the INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION is considered firm "everywhere," the Security Coordinator may attempt to arrange for Transmission Providers or Control Areas to redispatch generation or reconfigure transmission, even if they are off the contract path, to try to avoid curtailing the INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION that is using Firm Transmission Service. If agreements are in place to provide for redispatch to relieve the Constraint, these agreements should be evaluated by the Security Coordinator before asking others to redispatch.

 

Examples

This section explains by example the obligations of the Transmission Providers on and off the contract path regarding reconfiguring transmission, redispatching generation, and calling for Transmission Loading Relief. (References to Principles refer to Section C, "Principles for Mitigating Constraints On and Off the Contract Path," on the preceding pages.

Scenario:

  • INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION arranged from system A to system D
  • Contract path is A-E-C-D (except as noted)
  • Locations 1 and 2 denote Constraints

 

Case 1: E is a non-firm path, C is firm; E has Constraint at #2.
  • E is not obligated to redispatch generation or reconfigure transmission to mitigate Constraint #2. (Principle 1) Although C is providing Firm Service, the Constraint is not on C’s system, and C is not obligated to redispatch generation or reconfigure its transmission system to mitigate the Constraint on E’s system.
  • E may call Security Coordinator for TLR Procedure to relieve overload at Constraint #2. (Req. C.3.4.1.)
  • INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION A-D may be curtailed by TLR action as though it was being served by Non-firm Hourly Transmission Service, even though it was using firm service from C. That is, it takes on the lowest priority within the contract path. (Principle 1)

 

Case 2: E is a non-firm path, C is firm B has Constraint at #1.
  • B may call Security Coordinator for TLR Procedure to relieve overload at Constraint #1. (Req. C. 3.4.1.)
  • B is not obligated to redispatch generation or reconfigure transmission to mitigate Constraint at Constraint #1. (Principle 3)
  • INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION A-D may be curtailed by TLR action as though it was being served by Non-firm Hourly Transmission Service, even if it was using firm Transmission Service elsewhere on the path. That is, it takes on the lowest priority within the contract path. (Principle 3)

 

Case 3: E is a firm path A, D, and C are Non-firm; E has Constraint at #2.
  • INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION A – D is considered Firm priority for curtailment purposes.
  • E may then call Security Coordinator for TLR, which would curtail all non-firm INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs first. (Req. C. 3.4.1.)
  • E is obligated to redispatch generation or reconfigure transmission to mitigate Constraint #2 in E before E may curtail the INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION as ordered by the TLR. (Principle 2)

 

Case 4: The entire path (A-E-C-D) is firm; E has Constraint at #2.
  • INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION A – D is considered Firm priority for curtailment purposes.
  • E may call Security Coordinator for TLR. (Req. C.3.4.1.)
  • E is obligated to curtail non-firm INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs, and then redispatch generation or reconfigure transmission on its system, or, if there is an agreement in place, arrange for redispatch or reconfiguration on another system, to mitigate Constraint #2 in E before the firm A-D transaction is curtailed. (Principle 2)
  • A, C, D, may be requested by E to redispatch generation or reconfigure transmission to mitigate Constraint #2 in E at E’s expense. (Principle 2)

 

Case 5: The entire path (A-E-C-D) is firm; B has Constraint at #1.
  • INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION A – D is considered Firm priority for curtailment purposes.
  • B may call Security Coordinator for TLR Procedure for all non-firm INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs that contribute to the overload at Constraint #1. (Req. C. 3.4.1.)
  • Following the curtailment of all non-firm INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs, the Security Coordinator(s) will determine which Control Area(s) will redispatch their generation or reconfigure their transmission to mitigate constraint #1. (Principle 4)
  • A-D transaction may be curtailed as a result. However, the A-D transaction is treated as a firm INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION and will be curtailed only after non-firm INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs. (Note: This means that the firm contract path is respected by all parties, including those not on the contract path.) (Principle 4)

 

Case 6: Two A-to-D transactions using A-B-C-D and A-E-C-D; A and B are non-firm; B has Constraint at #1
  • B is not obligated to redispatch generation or reconfigure transmission to mitigate Constraint at #1. (Principle 1)
  • B may call for TLR Procedure to relieve overload at Constraint #1. (Req. C.3.4.1)
  • Both A – D INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs are subject to curtailment because they have the same PTDF across Constraint #1. However, INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION A – D using the A-B-C-D path is assigned a higher priority (NM), and would not be curtailed until after the Transaction using the path A-E-C-D (NH).

 

D. Transaction Curtailment Formula

Example

This example is based on the premise that a transaction should be curtailed in proportion to its PTDF on the Constraints. Its effect on the interface is a combination of its size in MW and its effect based on its distribution factor.

Column

Description

1. Initial Transaction

 		INTERCHANGE TRANSACTION before the TLR Procedure is implemented.

2. Distribution Factor

 		Proportional effect of the Transaction over the constrained interface due to the physical arrangement and impedance of the transmission system.

3. Impact on the Interface

 		Result of multiplying the Transaction MW by the distribution factor. This yields the MW that flow through the constrained interface from the Transaction. Performing this calculation for each Transaction yields the total flow through the constrained interface from all the INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs. In this case, 760 MW.

4. Impact Weighting Factor

 		"Normalization" of the total of the Distribution Factors in Column 2. Calculated by dividing the Distribution Factor for each Transaction by the total of the Distribution Factors.

5. Weighted Maximum Interface Reduction

 		Multiplying the Impact on the Interface from each Transaction by its Impact Weighting Factor yields a new proportion that is a combination of the MW Impact on the Interface and the Distribution Factor.

6. Interface Reduction

 		Multiplying the amount we need to reduce the flow over the constrained interface (280 MW) by the normalization of the Weighted Maximum Interface Reduction yields the actual MW reduction that each Transaction must contribute to achieve the total reduction.

7. Transaction Reduction

 		Now we have to divide by the Distribution Factor to see how much the Transaction must be reduced to yield the result we calculated in Column 7. Note that the reductions for the first two INTERCHANGE TRANSACTIONs (A-D (1) and A-D (2) are in proportion to their size since their distribution factors are equal.

8. New Transaction Amount

 		Subtracting the Transaction Reduction from the Initial Transaction yields the New Transaction Amount.

9. Adjusted Impact on Interface

 		A check to ensure the new constrained interface MW flow has been reduced to the target amount.
